About Me

Hi, I'm Rohit Balaji. I’m a Part II Software Engineering student at the University of Auckland with a passion for building immersive full-stack experiences. Inspired by the depth of virtual worlds in gaming, I transitioned from playing to creating, focusing on seamless front-end interfaces and robust back-end systems.

I build tools to solve real-world frustrations. Driven by empathy and adaptability, I strive to create software that makes life easier for users. I actively explore emerging technologies to ensure I’m building the most efficient and effective solutions possible.

Dazey - AI Journal & Note Taker

Dazey is an intelligent AI powered, privacy-first journaling and note-taking app that helps bring clarity and structure to your thoughts. Using local AI(Gemma 3n 4b), it organizes raw thoughts, notes, and reflections into smart categories, detects habit patterns, and includes a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) chatbot for searching past notes. Built for the GDGCxDEVS Hackathon 2025 by a team of me along with five other second-year students, Dazey won First Place as well as the Emerging Talent award.
